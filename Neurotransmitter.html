1. Tyrosine derivatives

  • Chemical Structure:Tyrosine is an aromatic amino acid with a hydroxyl group. Its molecular formula is C₉H₁₁N₃O₃.
  • Non-Essential Amino Acid: Unlike essential amino acids, the body can synthesize tyrosine from phenylalanine, another amino acid
  • Precursor to Neurotransmitters: Tyrosine is a precursor for several important neurotransmitters, including dopamine, norepinephrine, and epinephrine (adrenaline).
  • Role in Protein Synthesis: Tyrosine is incorporated into proteins and is important for maintaining various bodily functions
  • Solubility: Tyrosine is soluble in water and slightly soluble in alcohol, allowing it to be easily absorbed in the digestive tract.

Applications of tyrosine derivatives

  • Dietary Supplements: Tyrosine is available as a dietary supplement, often marketed for its potential to enhance mood, cognitive function, and stress response.
  • Cognitive Function: Studies suggest that tyrosine supplementation may improve cognitive flexibility and working memory, particularly under stress

2. Tryptophan Derivatives

  • Chemical Structure: Tryptophan is classified as a large, aromatic amino acid, featuring an indole side chain. Its molecular formula is C₁₁H₁₂N₂O₂.
  • Essential Amino Acid: It cannot be synthesized by the human body and must be obtained through diet
  • Precursor for Neurotransmitters: Tryptophan is a precursor to serotonin, a neurotransmitter that regulates mood, sleep, and appetite. It also converts into melatonin, which regulates sleep-wake cycles.
  • Role in Protein Synthesis: Like other amino acids, tryptophan is a building block for proteins
  • Absorption and Metabolism: Tryptophan is absorbed in the small intestine and can be converted into several bioactive compounds, including serotonin and niacin (vitamin B3).

Applications of tryptophan derivatives

  • Dietary Supplements: Tryptophan is commonly used in supplements aimed at improving mood, reducing anxiety, and promoting sleep
  • Sleep disorders:Due to its role in melatonin production, tryptophan is sometimes used to help treat sleep disorders, including insomnia.

3. Histidine derivatives

  • Chemical Structure: Histidine is an amino acid characterized by an imidazole side chain, which can be positively charged depending on the pH.
  • Molecular Formula: C₆H₉N₃O₂
  • Molar Mass: Approximately 155.16 g/mol
  • Solubility: Histidine is soluble in water, which is important for its biological functions
  • pKa Values: The side chain has a pKa around 6.0, meaning it can be protonated at physiological pH, making it a good buffer.
  • Appearance: Typically appears as a white crystalline powder
  • Melting point: The melting point is approximately 287 °C.
  • Taste and odour: Histidine is generally tasteless and odorless in its pure form.

Applications of Histidine

  • Nutritional Supplements: Histidine is available as a dietary supplement, often aimed at supporting muscle health and recovery.
  • Pharmaceuticals: It is used in various drug formulations and as a stabilizing agent in protein-based therapies
Derivative Function Key Property
GABA Neurotransmitter Inhibitory Role: GABA reduces neuronal excitability by hyperpolarizing neurons, making them less likely to fire action potentials

3. Glutamate derivatives

  • Neurotransmitter Function: Glutamate is the most abundant excitatory neurotransmitter in the central nervous system, playing a crucial role in synaptic transmission, learning, and memory.
  • Protein Synthesis: As a non-essential amino acid, glutamate is a key component in the synthesis of proteins
  • Metabolic Pathways: It is involved in various metabolic pathways, including the Krebs cycle (citric acid cycle), contributing to energy production
  • Detoxification: Glutamate plays a role in detoxifying ammonia in the brain by converting it to glutamine
  • Cell Signaling: It influences cell signaling pathways, including those related to cell growth and differentiation.
  • Role in Immune Function: Glutamate can modulate immune responses and influence the activity of certain immune cells.
  • Antioxidant Role: It is involved in the synthesis of glutathione, a key antioxidant that helps protect cells from oxidative damage.
  • Acid-Base Balance: Glutamate participates in maintaining acid-base balance in the body by acting as a buffer

Application of glutamate derivatives

  • Flavor Enhancer: Glutamate is widely used as a flavor enhancer (monosodium glutamate, or MSG) in foods to enhance umami taste, commonly found in processed foods, soups, and snacks.
  • Amino Acid Supplement: It is included in dietary supplements aimed at promoting muscle recovery, improving athletic performance, and supporting overall health.
