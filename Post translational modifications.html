Introduction

Post-Translational Modifications (PTMs) are chemical changes made to proteins after they are synthesized during translation. These modifications play a critical role in regulating protein function, localization, stability, and interactions.

Examples

  • Lysine:Acetyllysine
  • Proline:Hydroxyline
  • Serine:Phosphoserine

1. Lysine

  • Chemical Structure:Lysine is an α-amino acid with the chemical formula C₆H₁₄N₂O₂. It has an amino group (-NH₂)
  • Essential Amino Acid:It cannot be synthesized by the body and must be obtained through diet, making it essential for growth and tissue repair
  • Hydrophilic:Due to its polar nature, lysine is water-soluble, which is important for its transport and function in the body
  • pKa Values:Lysine has multiple pKa values, which affect its ionization state and solubility depending on the pH of the environment

Applications:

  • Nutritional Supplement
  • Viral Infection Management

2. Proline

  • Chemical Structure and Formula:: Proline is classified as a non-essential amino acid with the chemical formula C₅H₉N₁O₂. Its structure includes a five-membered ring formed by the amino group and the side chain, making it a cyclic amino acid
  • Non-Essential Amino AcidWhile proline can be synthesized by the body, it can also be obtained through diet, primarily from protein sources
  • Unique Conformation:The cyclic structure of proline introduces rigidity in polypeptides, influencing protein folding and stability
  • Hydrophobic Character:Proline is generally hydrophobic, although it can also exhibit polar characteristics due to its amine group, allowing it to play diverse roles in protein structures

Applications

  • Collagen Production
  • Proline is used in dietary supplements to support joint health

3. Serine

  • Chemical Structure:Serine has the chemical formula C₃H₇N₁O₃. It contains a hydroxymethyl group (-CH₂OH) in its side chain, which makes it polar and hydrophilic
  • Non-Essential Amino Acid:The body can synthesize serine from other compounds, meaning it does not need to be obtained solely through diet
  • Hydrophilic Nature:Due to its polar characteristics, serine is soluble in water and plays an important role in protein interactions and enzymatic reactions.
  • Role in Protein Structure:Serine can participate in hydrogen bonding, which contributes to protein stability and folding
  • Phosphorylation:It can undergo post-translational modification through phosphorylation, affecting protein function and signaling pathways

Applications

  • Promoting cognitive function
  • Contributes to the synthesis of D-serine, a co-agonist for the NMDA receptor
