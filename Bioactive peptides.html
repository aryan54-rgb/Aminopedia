Examples

  • Angiotensinogen: Angiotensin
  • Kininogen:Bradykinin
  • Proinsulin: Insulin

1. Physical properties

  1. Chemical Structure:
    • Angiotensin I: A decapeptide (10 amino acids), with the sequence: Asp-Arg-Val-Tyr-Ile-His-Pro-Phe-Thr-Ser.
    • Angiotensin II: An octapeptide (8 amino acids), with the sequence: Asp-Arg-Val-Tyr-Ile-His-Pro-Phe
    • Angiotensin is a peptide composed of several amino acids. The most well-known forms are Angiotensin I and Angiotensin II.
  2. Molecular weight:
    • Angiotensin I has a molecular weight of approximately 1,073 Da.
    • Angiotensin II has a molecular weight of about 1,046 Da.
  3. Solubility:
    • Angiotensin peptides are soluble in water.

2.Biological properties
